Activity 2 Questions & Answers:

2.1. Why are the variables declared as private?
Answer: Variables are declared as private to restrict direct access from outside the class. This implements the concept of "Data Hiding" and Encapsulation, ensuring that data cannot be modified arbitrarily or accidentally by other parts of the program.

2.2. What happens if the variables are declared public?
Answer: If the variables are declared public, any external class can directly access and modify their values without any control or validation (e.g., setting a negative CGPA). This violates OOP safety principles.

2.3. Why do we use getters and setters?
Answer: We use getters and setters to provide a controlled public interface for accessing (getting) and modifying (setting) the private data. This allows us to add validation logic in the future if needed, maintaining control over how data is handled.
